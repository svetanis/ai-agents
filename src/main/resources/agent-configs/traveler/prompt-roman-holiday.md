## ACTIVITY
Here's a one-day itinerary for your solo trip to Rome, focusing on history, museums, galleries, and cathedrals, with flexible dates at the end of May 2026:

Rome: A Day of Ancient Wonders and Baroque Beauty
Morning: Ancient Rome's Grandeur

Colosseum: Step back in time at this iconic amphitheater, once the stage for gladiatorial contests and public spectacles.
Estimated cost: €18 (approx. $20 USD) for a combined ticket with the Roman Forum and Palatine Hill.
Roman Forum & Palatine Hill: Explore the sprawling ruins of ancient Rome's political, religious, and commercial center, and ascend Palatine Hill for panoramic views of the city.
Estimated cost: Included with the Colosseum ticket.
Lunch Recommendation (near Colosseum):

Alle Carrette: A highly-rated pizzeria known for its authentic Roman-style pizzas and casual atmosphere, perfect for a quick and delicious lunch.
Afternoon: Architectural Marvels and Baroque Art

Pantheon: Marvel at the architectural genius of this remarkably preserved ancient Roman temple, now a church, famous for its massive unreinforced concrete dome and oculus.
Estimated cost: Free (timed entry may be required, check closer to your travel date).
Piazza Navona: Stroll through one of Rome's most beautiful Baroque squares, adorned with Bernini's Fountain of Four Rivers and other impressive sculptures and fountains.
Estimated cost: Free.
Evening: Authentic Italian Dinner

Armando al Pantheon: Indulge in classic Roman cuisine at this beloved trattoria, renowned for its traditional dishes and proximity to the Pantheon.
Estimated cost: €40-60 (approx. $44-66 USD) for a full meal with wine.
